# Legal Compliance and Regulatory Information

## ⚠️ CRITICAL LEGAL NOTICE

**The use of active RF countermeasures (jamming, protocol injection, deauthentication) is STRICTLY REGULATED and ILLEGAL in most jurisdictions without explicit authorization.**

## International Regulations

### United States

**Federal Communications Commission (FCC)**:
- **47 U.S.C. § 333**: Prohibits willful or malicious interference with radio communications
- **47 CFR § 15.5(b)**: Bans marketing of jamming devices
- **Penalties**: Up to $112,500 per violation, criminal prosecution, imprisonment

**Authorized Use**:
- Federal law enforcement with warrant
- Military operations
- Critical infrastructure protection (with FCC Special Temporary Authority)

**Reference**: [FCC Enforcement Advisory on Jamming](https://www.fcc.gov/general/jammer-enforcement)

### European Union

**Radio Equipment Directive (RED) 2014/53/EU**:
- Article 3: Essential requirements for radio equipment
- Jamming devices prohibited under national laws

**Member State Regulations**:
- **UK**: Wireless Telegraphy Act 2006 - Up to 2 years imprisonment
- **Germany**: TKG § 55 - Fines up to €500,000
- **France**: ARCEP regulations - Criminal penalties

### Russian Federation

**Federal Law No. 126-FZ** (On Communications):
- Article 23: Prohibition of interference with radio communications
- **Penalties**: Administrative fines, equipment confiscation

**Authorized Use**:
- FSB and military operations
- Critical infrastructure protection with Roskomnadzor approval

### Other Jurisdictions

- **Canada**: Radiocommunication Act - Fines up to CAD $5,000,000
- **Australia**: Radiocommunications Act 1992 - Up to 2 years imprisonment
- **China**: Radio Regulation of PRC - Administrative detention
- **Japan**: Radio Act Article 4 - Up to 1 year imprisonment

## Authorized Use Cases

### 1. Military and Defense

**Requirements**:
- Active military personnel or contractors
- Operational authorization from commanding officer
- Compliance with Rules of Engagement (ROE)
- Frequency coordination with spectrum management

**Documentation**:
- Mission orders
- Spectrum allocation certificates
- Equipment type approval

### 2. Law Enforcement

**Requirements**:
- Sworn law enforcement officer
- Court-issued warrant or exigent circumstances
- Compliance with agency policies
- Post-operation reporting

**Applicable Scenarios**:
- Hostage situations
- Prison security
- Counter-terrorism operations
- Bomb disposal (drone-borne IEDs)

### 3. Critical Infrastructure Protection

**Requirements**:
- Facility security clearance
- Regulatory authority approval (FCC STA, etc.)
- Defined protection zone
- Interference mitigation plan
- 24/7 monitoring and logging

**Eligible Facilities**:
- Nuclear power plants
- Government buildings
- Airports (with FAA coordination)
- Prisons

### 4. Research and Development

**Requirements**:
- Licensed research facility
- Anechoic chamber or RF-shielded environment
- No emissions outside controlled area
- Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval if applicable

**Documentation**:
- Experimental license
- Test plan and protocols
- Safety procedures

## Compliance Checklist

Before enabling countermeasures, verify:

- [ ] Legal authorization obtained from regulatory authority

- [ ] Frequency coordination completed
- [ ] Interference mitigation plan in place
- [ ] Logging and reporting procedures established
- [ ] Personnel trained on legal constraints

## Passive Detection (Legal)

**Passive RF monitoring (receive-only mode) is generally legal** for:

- Personal security research
- Educational purposes
- Hobbyist experimentation
- Property protection (monitoring only)

**No authorization required** when:
- No transmissions are made
- No interference is caused
- Monitoring is within your property
- No privacy laws are violated

## Ethical Considerations

**Even when legal, consider**:

1. **Proportionality**: Is jamming necessary, or are other measures sufficient?
2. **Collateral Damage**: Will countermeasures affect innocent communications?
3. **Escalation**: Could your actions provoke dangerous responses?
4. **Privacy**: Are you monitoring communications you shouldn't?

## Recommended Alternatives

**Legal drone countermeasures**:

1. **Physical barriers**: Nets, cages, fencing
2. **Visual deterrents**: Signage, lighting
3. **Acoustic deterrents**: Ultrasonic emitters
4. **Geofencing**: Coordinate with drone manufacturers
5. **Law enforcement**: Report violations to authorities
